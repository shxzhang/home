Research

My main research interested right now is in plant resiliency to climate change. Specifically, while there is extensive amount of research about the response of Arabidopsis to various types of abiotic stress such as heat, drought, salinity, etc., there is limited knowledge about how perennial crops, especially trees, would respond to these challenges. Furthermore, many of these challenges, such as drought and soil salinization, occur together under field conditions. I am particularly interested in what happens if stresses that when applied individually would result in opposing changes occur together.

Currently my work mainly focuses on pistachios, a woody perennial crop known for its relatively high salinity and drought tolerance. Much less is known about abiotic stress tolerance in woody crops compared to annual crops or Arabidopsis due to their long generation time and difficulty in micropropagation / in-bred lines. However, for the same reasons, their long life span means that it is likely that woody plants will employ different mechanisms for resiliency compared to annuals, and the popularity of crops such as pistachios, almonds, walnuts, as well as many fruits, means that there is a pressing need to understand climate change resiliency for these crops.

THE DARK CYCLE

THE DARK CYCLE is a instructional boardgame about photosynthesis for undergraduate and higher levels of education.
(It comes with 2 levels of game play, simple and advanced. Advanced is recommended for people who major / work in biology / biology related fields.)

THE ACCUSED

THE THE ACCUSED is a murder mystery game suitable for undergraduate laboratory material that involves identification of samples (e.g. identifying cell types, soil type, plant type).

It is originally developed for the PLB105 Plant Anatomy course at UC Davis and can be adapted easily for any course material that contains identification via dichotomous keying.

In addition to providing a fun way to engage with course material, the game provides one way for the students to appreciate the importance of paying attention to details, the relevancy of being able to identify key samples (especially in the context of forensic science), and the logic of dichotomous keying
